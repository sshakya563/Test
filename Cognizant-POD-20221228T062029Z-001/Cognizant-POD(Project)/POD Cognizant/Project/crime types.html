Crime Types

Personal Crimes
Personal crimes, which are committed by an offender against another person. Examples of these crimes include homicide, assault, battery, rape, sexual assault, and more. In this, the victims themselves are directly harmed by the offender.
Statutory Crimes
Statutory offenses refer to behavior that is made illegal by laws passed by a governing body, like the legislature, as opposed to behavior that violates the common law—or “judge-made law”. Usually these are defined under the criminal code.
Hate Crimes
A hate crime (also known as a bias-motivated crime or bias crime)is a prejudice-motivated crime which occurs when a perpetrator targets a victim because of their membership (or perceived membership) of a certain social group or racial demographic.
Modern Slavery

Cyber Crimes
Cybercrime is criminal activity that either targets or uses a computer, a computer network or a networked device.Most, but not all, cybercrime is committed by cybercriminals or hackers who want to make money. It is carried out by individuals or organizations.
Felony Crimes
Felonies are the most serious crimes you can commit and have long jail or prison sentences, fines, or permanent loss of freedoms. A felony may be punishable with imprisonment for two or more years or death in the case of the most serious felonies.
